## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Positioning options
The differentiation-strategy deliverable SHALL present 3–5 distinct positioning options for Ravi Sweets. Each option MUST be a single sentence in the form "For <target customer>, Ravi Sweets is the <category> that <unique claim>, because <proof>."

At least one option MUST target **Hyderabad-local retail/gifting**, at least one MUST target **corporate / B2B gifting**, and at least one MUST target **diaspora / international** customers, so the strategy set covers the three stated growth vectors.

#### Scenario: Options are distinct
- **WHEN** the options are reviewed
- **THEN** no two options collapse to the same target-customer + unique-claim pair

#### Scenario: Each option names its target customer
- **WHEN** a positioning option is read
- **THEN** the target customer is concretely described (e.g., "Hyderabad-based professionals buying festival gifts for family", "Bengaluru/Gurgaon HR teams placing 500+ unit Diwali corporate orders", "US-based second-generation Indian families ordering for Diwali"), not generic ("sweet lovers")

#### Scenario: Growth-vector coverage is present
- **WHEN** the option set is reviewed
- **THEN** at least one option each addresses Hyderabad retail, corporate/B2B, and diaspora/international segments

### Requirement: Each option is grounded in the benchmark
Every positioning option SHALL cite at least one whitespace area from the market-benchmark and at least one dimension where Ravi Sweets already scores at or above tier median (a defendable strength to build from).

#### Scenario: Whitespace citation is explicit
- **WHEN** a positioning option is justified
- **THEN** it names the specific whitespace dimension(s) and the competitors scoring ≤ 2 there

### Requirement: Each option names what it is NOT
Each option SHALL explicitly name 2–3 named competitors it is deliberately NOT trying to be, and what it sacrifices by choosing this position.

#### Scenario: Sacrifice is named
- **WHEN** a positioning option is reviewed
- **THEN** the trade-off ("we will not try to beat X on price / national reach / SKU breadth") is stated

### Requirement: Recommended option with rationale
The deliverable SHALL pick ONE recommended option, explain why it wins over the others on a short scorecard (fit with current strengths, whitespace size, execution difficulty, defensibility, time-to-signal), and name the top 2 risks with mitigations.

#### Scenario: Recommendation has a scorecard
- **WHEN** the recommended option is presented
- **THEN** the five-criterion scorecard (fit / whitespace size / execution difficulty / defensibility / time-to-signal) is filled for all options considered

### Requirement: Proof-point inventory for the recommended option
The recommended option SHALL be accompanied by a list of concrete proof points Ravi Sweets would need to stand up to make the claim credible (e.g., specific SKUs, ingredient claims, packaging cues, content pillars, store-experience changes, certifications).

#### Scenario: Proof points are testable
- **WHEN** a proof point is listed
- **THEN** it is specific enough that a third party could verify whether it exists (e.g., "3 gifting SKUs in the ₹499–₹999 band with festival-specific packaging" vs. "better gifting")

### Requirement: Positioning anti-patterns
The deliverable SHALL include a short "anti-patterns" list — positioning moves Ravi Sweets should avoid given the competitive set (e.g., "don't chase national heritage narrative against Haldiram's", "don't go low-price against Bikaji MRP-led scale").

#### Scenario: Anti-patterns cite the competitor they would lose to
- **WHEN** an anti-pattern is listed
- **THEN** it names the competitor(s) that dominate that position and why chasing them is unwinnable
