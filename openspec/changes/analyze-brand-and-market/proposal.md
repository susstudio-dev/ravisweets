## Why

Ravi Sweets (ravisweets.com) is a **Hyderabad-based** traditional sweets & namkeen brand with two growth ambitions: (a) **global expansion** — reach the Indian diaspora in the US, UK, Middle East, Australia, Canada, and Singapore — and (b) **corporate / B2B gifting** as a first-class revenue line. It competes against a stacked field: pan-India heritage giants (Haldiram's, Bikanervala, Bikaji, MTR), entrenched Hyderabad players (Pulla Reddy, Almond House, Karachi Bakery, G. Pulla Reddy Sweets, Mahalakshmi Sweets, Agarwal Sweets, Bombay Halwa House), modern D2C/premium gifting brands (Bombay Sweet Shop, Anand Sweets' modern line), and diaspora-facing international shippers (Haldiram's international, Bikaji exports, Royal Sweets USA, Ambala Sweets UK, Mithaas). To grow share, win corporate accounts, defend pricing power, and justify international go-to-market spend, Ravi Sweets needs an evidence-based picture of its own digital/brand footprint AND a structured map of who the competition is — domestically and in diaspora markets — what they do well, and where the whitespace lies. Without this, product, packaging, pricing, corporate-gifting, and international-channel decisions are being made on gut feel.

## What Changes

- Conduct a **precision audit of ravisweets.com** — catalogue every page, product, price, category, claim, delivery/shipping policy (including international shipping if any), origin story, founder narrative, certifications, imagery style, SEO metadata, tech stack, and customer-engagement surfaces (reviews, WhatsApp, contact, newsletter), with explicit attention to any existing corporate-gifting or international-order paths.
- Build a **four-tier competitor landscape dossier** — (1) National giants, (2) Hyderabad regional players, (3) D2C / premium gifting-first disruptors, (4) **Diaspora / international shippers** serving Indian customers abroad — capturing product mix, signature SKUs, pricing bands (domestic ₹ and where applicable international $/£/AED), packaging, shelf-life claims, gifting propositions, **corporate / B2B gifting offering (yes/no + notes)**, distribution (own stores, quick-commerce, Amazon/Flipkart, own D2C, international shipping, diaspora retail partners), content/social strategy, and recent campaigns.
- Produce a **comparative benchmark** (Ravi Sweets vs. competitors) across product breadth, pricing, packaging, digital maturity, storytelling, trust signals, gifting/B2B capability, channel coverage, **and international/export readiness**.
- Deliver a **differentiation & positioning recommendation** — 3–5 concrete, defendable ways Ravi Sweets can stand out (e.g., Hyderabad Nizami-heritage authenticity, clean-label/no-preservative, premium corporate-gifting range, diaspora-first gifting with international-shipping guarantees, sugar-conscious line, quick-commerce play, festival calendar marketing).
- Deliver a **go-to-market action list** — prioritised, sequenced, with owner-ready briefs covering **Quick Wins, 30/60/90, and Strategic Bets** (including corporate-gifting programme, international-shipping enablement, and first-diaspora-market launch).
- **Non-goal**: This change does NOT rebuild the website, launch new SKUs, or run paid campaigns. It produces the research, benchmark, and strategy deck that those downstream changes will be scoped from.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities
- `brand-audit`: Structured, evidence-backed audit of Ravi Sweets' own digital presence (website, catalogue, pricing, story, trust signals, tech, SEO, CX touchpoints).
- `competitor-intelligence`: Researched dossier of national, regional, and D2C competitors with normalised data fields so side-by-side comparison is possible.
- `market-benchmark`: Comparative scorecard that places Ravi Sweets against the competitive set on a defined set of dimensions.
- `differentiation-strategy`: Prioritised positioning options and a recommended "how we win" direction with rationale and risks.
- `gtm-action-plan`: Sequenced, owner-ready action list (quick wins, 30/60/90, strategic bets) derived from the benchmark + strategy.

## Impact

- **Deliverables produced**: Markdown reports under `openspec/changes/analyze-brand-and-market/` and, post-archive, spec files under `openspec/specs/` describing each capability's outputs and acceptance criteria.
- **Downstream changes unlocked**: website redesign/SEO fixes, packaging refresh, new SKU development (gifting, sugar-conscious, regional heritage), channel expansion (quick-commerce, Amazon), content/social calendar, B2B/corporate-gifting playbook.
- **Dependencies**: public web research only (competitor websites, marketplaces, social, press, Play Store reviews, Google Maps reviews). No paid data sources required in v1; a v2 may add Similarweb / Meta Ad Library / Google Trends for quantitative depth.
- **Stakeholders**: founder/owner (direction), marketing (execution), product/kitchen (SKU decisions), ops (channel/logistics).
- **Risks**: competitor data is point-in-time and may drift; pricing/SKU info on third-party sites may lag in-store reality; regional players have thin digital footprints and will need phone/in-person validation flagged as follow-ups.
